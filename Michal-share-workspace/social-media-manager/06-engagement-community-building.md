# Step 6 — Engagement and Community Building

## ⚠️ Privacy applies here too, not just in the Gems

This step explicitly invites sharing "insights from clients" into public content. The same
red line from `gems/privacy-preamble.md` applies: **no identifying detail, ever** — and that
includes indirectly identifying combinations (age + role + a specific quote can de-anonymize
someone in a small community, even with no name attached). Keep client-derived content at the
**pattern level only** — "a feeling I hear often," never a recognizable individual story.

## Comment response style

Validate the feeling first, never deliver a full diagnosis or advice in a public comment
(boundary + liability, and it funnels real depth to DM/course instead of giving it away free
in a thread). One to two sentences, in her warm-direct voice.

Template:
> "תודה שכתבת/ה את זה. זה ממש מוכר ולא קל. [תגובה קצרה, לא ייעוץ מלא] — אם תרצה/י להעמיק בזה, אני כאן."

## DM opener templates (not pushy — earn the conversation first)

- Someone comments emotionally: "ראיתי את התגובה שלך, ורציתי קודם כל להגיד — את לא לבד בזה. 💛"
- Someone asks a direct question: "שאלה טובה. אפשר לכתוב לך תשובה קצרה כאן, או אם תרצה/י לדבר על זה יותר בעומק אני כאן."
- **Avoid:** leading with a course pitch in the first DM, ever. Earn the conversation first — matches her explicit "not pushy" preference.

## Collaboration templates

Identify partners who are complementary, not competing: couples therapists, family
photographers, parenting coaches, somatic practitioners. Propose a joint Reel or Live on an
overlapping topic (e.g. "boundaries with parents/in-laws") — cross-pollinates audiences without
either side feeling like competition.

## Daily 30-minute engagement routine

- **10 min** — reply to comments from the last 48 hours
- **10 min** — initiate 3–5 DMs with new followers or highly-engaged commenters (using the openers above)
- **10 min** — genuinely engage on 5–10 other accounts in her niche (real comments, not generic "🔥" drops)

## Turning followers into fans

- Ask for replies, not just likes — captions should end in a real question sometimes, not always a CTA.
- Share anonymized session insights **at the pattern level only** (see privacy note above).
- Celebrate community milestones (follower counts, "this helped someone" moments) — emotional, not promotional.
